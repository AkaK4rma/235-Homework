Geomancy

A Tactical Grid Based Bullet-Hell

High Concept

Geomancy is a high-contrast, tactical bullet-hell played on a 4x5 fixed grid. The player controls a wizard avatar and combines movement, an invincible dash, and a Siphon mechanic (similar to Ikaruga's polarity switching) to survive and defeat a massive, single-screen Evil Tree. The project's unique "Cute/Dark" aesthetic blends appealing character design with demanding, high-performance gameplay.

Genre

Boss-Rush, Grid-Based Tactical Action, Fixed-Screen Bullet Hell.

Platform

Desktop Web (PC/Mac). The project will utilize the PixiJS library, leveraging WebGL acceleration capable of efficient, high-frame-rate rendering of hundreds of moving bullet sprites and particle effects.

Story

The player takes on the role of a Vizard, a wizard who was outcast from the academy and forced to study on their own. After being outcast, their studies led them to adopt a vagabond lifestyle, leading them to areas unknown.

During these journeys, they encountered many a foe, but none as strong as the Root, a massive tree that attacks using its roots, branches, and apples. The narrative is concise but open. It allows the focus to be on the moment-to-moment challenge of survival and achieving a low time to beat the boss.

Aesthetics

The project employs a "Cute/Dark" aesthetic, striking a strong visual contrast.

  • The Avatar: Small and expressive, designed to evoke a Kirby-like appeal.
  • The Boss: The softness is carried along to the Root Boss, a large tree that seems unassuming at first but attempts to snuff out the life of those who challenge it.
  • The World: The background is a simple field. The 4x5 grid lines use a subtle, pastel glow. Bullet sprites are simple, distinct geometric shapes (apples and roots).
  • Feedback: To clearly denote their Siphon color type, attacks will have different colors, and grids will flash the color of the attack to be absorbed.

Gameplay & Mechanics

The game is a single-screen, single-boss encounter designed to test tactical precision.

The Core Loop

  1. Player Input: Press WASD (Move), Space/LMB (Attack), Shift (Dash), or Z/RMB (Siphon).
  2. Game Action: The Vizard moves or fires. The Root Boss executes its current phase's attack script (Red/Blue bullets).
  3. Feedback: Score increases, boss health drops, audio cues play, or the Super Attack Meter charges.

Core Mechanics

  • Grid Movement: Player moves one square at a time on the 4x5 grid, with Horizontal Grid Wrap to instantly reposition to the other side.
  • Quick Dash (Shift): Instantly moves two squares with a crucial 0.5s of Invulnerability on a fixed cooldown.
  • Siphon (Z/RMB): Absorbs same-colored bullets by running into them to charge the Super Attack Meter. Hitting opposite-colored bullets results in damage.

Scope & MVP

The scope is constrained to a single boss to allow for deep polish.

MVP Focus

Ensuring core movement (Grid, Wrap, Dash), collision detection, and Phase 1 of the boss fight are perfect. The Siphon will be a simple heal/destroy mechanic initially.

Stretch Goals

  • Full Dual-Color Siphon/Super Attack Meter implementation.
  • Phase 2 and Phase 3 of the Boss (escalating difficulty).
