Project 3 Documentation

Geomancy - A Grid-Based Bullet-Hell

Concept & Overview

Geomancy is a bullet-hell game played on a grid. The player takes on the role of a vizard who must combine movement and a Siphon mechanic (similar to Ikaruga's polarity switching) to survive and defeat a massive Evil Tree.

Developer Notes & Reflections

I might not have been able to fully realize the concepts on the proposal, but I am very happy with how the game turned out.

Notes

  • After struggling to implement advanced screen effects (CRT filters, bloom, and other effects) for hours, I abandoned the idea and stuck to the default renderer. It sucks because I really wanted to add them.. But alas.
  • Time was a major issue. I had to work on this, as well as 2 other games and 1 other 3D modeling project simultaneously with similar due dates.
  • I don't know if the game is beatable right now as I have not been able to beat it after the final difficulty increase. This means I couldn't test the logic for the game's ending. The logic for winning was working before, but I made minor changes that I was not able to test before submission.

Development Process

Like Project 2, my process was characterized by trial and error. I leveraged my existing knowledge of the MonoGame framework to translate those skills over to the PixiJS library, which was quite similar.

Development Phases

  1. Core Gameplay First: I initially placed my focus on on main gameplay features like movement, shooting, and damage to have a concept ready for critique day.
  2. Feature Completion: After a short break to focus on other projects, the remaining features such as the completed siphon mechanic were completed.
  3. Aesthetics & Polish: After completing the features work shifted to fixing bugs, implementing aesthetics and animation, and playtesting while tweaking values until the game felt decent.
